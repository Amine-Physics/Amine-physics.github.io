Research paper: Eikonal Time Delay and Post-Newtonian Constraints in Bimetric Gravity

In this work, I extend the investigation of time delay effects in bimetric gravity. In my Master’s thesis, the photon was modeled as a spin-0 particle, justified by the fact that spin-dependent corrections are subleading in the classical limit. Here, I refine this analysis by explicitly incorporating the photon’s spin. The time delay is computed using stationary phase methods, and constraints on the parameter space of the theory are derived from solar system time delay measurements.

Research Internship: Sensitivity Study of the Wigner-Smith Operator: Applications in Electromagnetism

The Wigner-Smith (WS) operator, in its generalized form, serves as a powerful tool for identifying optimal wave shapes for specific tasks. These tasks can include minimizing dispersion while traversing a medium or focusing energy at a desired point. This operator relies on the derivative of the scattering matrix with respect to a variable of the interst (slab position, refractive index of a region, extera). Consequently, when using this operator in practice, interacting with the system could lead to other unwanted changes. This work proposes a simplified framework to investigate the impact of such unwanted changes on the behavior of the WS operator. The system under study is a 4-port system traversing a scattering region with a slab. First the eigenstates associated with a derivation with respect to the frequency and then the slab position are computed and obtained in simulation. Finally, we focus on the study of the Wigner-Smith operator when both changes are present. We demonstrate that the resulting eigenstates cannot be described as simple linear combinations of states arising from individual variations. Instead, the combined effect depends on the relative importance of the variations. This dependence can manifest as either a dominant influence from one variation or a more complex interplay between them.

Independent research project: Application Of The Wigner Smith Operator To Low Energy Meson Nucleon Interaction

This report investigates the application of the Wigner–Smith (WS) operator to low-energy meson–nucleon interactions within the framework of the Chew–Low model. The analysis is restricted to the one nucleonone- meson subspace and two interaction models are considered. In the first, nucleon recoil during meson emission or absorption is neglected; in the second, recoil effects are included in the nonrelativistic limit. To build analytical control over both models, the nucleon self-energy correction and the scattering matrix element are computed to second order in perturbation theory. The second part of the report focuses on the WS operator. After a brief literature review and theoretical overview, the operator is formulated within the restricted Hilbert space. An eigenvalue equation is derived in this setting with the aim of identifying physically meaningful eigenstates and interpreting their connection to the underlying dynamics
